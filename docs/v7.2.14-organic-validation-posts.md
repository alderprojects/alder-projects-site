## 4. Reddit (homeowner sub or r/vermont) — Basement moisture prep

**Title:**
What would you check before finishing a basement in an older Vermont house?

**Body:**

Asking the wisdom of people who've done this. I'm putting together a homeowner tool focused on the moisture-test step before finishing — and want to make sure I'm not missing something.

What I have so far for a 30-day pre-finish diagnostic:

- WiFi hygrometer in 2-3 corners (basement humidity vs upstairs is usually 20-30% higher in summer)
- Pin or pinless moisture meter on bottom 24" of every wall + framing
- Battery or WiFi water alarms at water heater, washer, sump, lowest floor point
- Properly-sized 50-pint dehumidifier with continuous drain
- DIY mold screening kit if there's a smell but no visible mold

Hard route-outs (skip the kit, call a pro):
- Active water during snowmelt or rain
- Visible mold over ~10 sq ft
- Foundation cracks > 1/8 inch, stair-step cracking, bowing walls
- Recurring humidity above 65% even with dehumidifier running

Specifically asking: what would experienced Vermont basement people add or remove from this list? And does the 30-day window feel right, or should it be longer?

Not selling anything in this post. If anyone wants to see the cart version: link in comments.
