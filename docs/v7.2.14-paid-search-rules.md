# v7.2.14 — Paid search rules

**Document only. Do not implement campaigns until the gating conditions below are met.**

The point of v7.2.14 is to validate organic pull before spending paid acquisition budget. Paid search waits for evidence that the pilots convert; once that exists, the rules below set the initial campaign parameters conservatively so the $19.99 cart economics don't break.

## Gating conditions — paid waits until ALL are true

- [ ] Organic visitors convert OR at least start checkout on at least one pilot scope (window weatherization or basement moisture prep)
- [ ] Result pages render credibly end-to-end (no 500s, no mis-priced picks, route-out logic visible)
- [ ] Route-out and contractor-lead paths fire events that land in the analytics dashboard (so we can see when paid traffic is misrouting)
- [ ] At least one pilot scope shows organic pull strong enough to justify amplification
- [ ] The decision-rule thresholds in [docs/v7.2.14-launch-gates.md](v7.2.14-launch-gates.md#decision-rule) are met (≥ 30 unique CTA clicks, ≥ 5 checkout starts, ≥ 2 purchases over a 14-day organic test)

If any of those is false, paid search is wrong. The fix is iterating on the organic pilots, not buying traffic to a funnel that doesn't convert.

## Economics guardrail

At $19.99 ACV, Smart Cart cannot tolerate high CPC unless conversion is very strong or the affiliate / contractor-lead upside is meaningful and trackable.

Rough breakeven math (sanity check, not a forecast):

- Cart fee: $19.99 (full)
- Estimated payment processing + variable cost: ~$1.50
- Net contribution per cart: ~$18.50
- Average affiliate revenue per cart (estimate): $5-15 (varies wildly by category)
- Total LTV per cart: ~$23-33

For paid search to break even on the cart fee alone, **CPA ≤ $18**. For CPA ≤ $9, click-to-purchase rate must be ≥ 50% (unrealistic for cold paid search) or CPC ≤ $1.

The realistic ceiling: **CPC ≤ $1.50 with click-to-purchase ≥ 5%**. That implies the cart needs to convert at 1 in 20 paid clicks, which depends on the targeting being tight.

## Initial campaign parameters (when ready to test)

### Match types

- **Exact match only.** No phrase match in the first 30 days.
- **No broad match. Ever.** Broad match on this ACV destroys the economics within 48 hours.

### Geography

- **Vermont only.** No "Vermont + neighboring states." The brand thesis depends on Vermont specificity; the pilots are tuned for Vermont buyers.
- **County-level exclusions** for low-population counties initially. Test Chittenden + Washington + Addison first (highest impression volume).

### Bid caps

- **Max CPC: $1.50.** Hard cap.
- **Daily budget: $30 for the first 7 days** (≤ 20 clicks/day).
- **No automated bidding** for the first 30 days. Manual CPC only — automated bidding learns on too little data and overpays.

### Keyword strategy

#### Window weatherization (when window pilot validates)

Recommended exact-match starting keywords:

- `window weatherization vermont`
- `window film vs replacement`
- `vermont window film`
- `winter window weatherization`
- `weatherization film for old windows`

Negative keywords (mandatory):

- `commercial`, `business`, `office`, `industrial`
- `replacement` (alone — too broad; "replacement vermont" is OK as exact-match negative qualifier)
- `installation`, `installer` (these are pro-funnel, not cart funnel)
- `free`, `cheap`, `diy guide` (free-info seekers don't convert)
- `film privacy`, `film tinted`, `film one way` (different product category — privacy films, not insulating films)

#### Basement moisture prep (when basement pilot validates)

Recommended exact-match starting keywords:

- `basement moisture vermont`
- `before finishing basement vermont`
- `vermont basement humidity`
- `basement dehumidifier vermont`
- `should i finish basement`

Negative keywords (mandatory):

- `commercial`, `business`
- `repair`, `waterproofing`, `flooded` (these go to pro funnel, not cart)
- `mold remediation`, `mold removal` (pro territory)
- `free`, `cheap`
- `cost to finish basement` (cost-research, not cart-buyer intent)

### Landing pages

**Send all paid traffic to topic landing pages, NOT the homepage.**

- Window keywords → `/smart-cart/topic/window-weatherization-vermont`
- Basement keywords → `/smart-cart/topic/basement-moisture-prep`

Why: the topic landing pages are conversion-tuned and pre-load the curation modal with topic + scope. Homepage forces the buyer to discover the product, which kills paid conversion rates.

### Ad copy guardrails

- Lead with the problem framing, not the price. ("Vermont winter drafts? Try this before replacement." beats "$19.99 product list — Vermont window weatherization.")
- Mention Vermont specifically in the headline. Geographic specificity drives quality score.
- Include the route-out as an honesty signal: "If your frames are rotted, we'll route you to a pro instead." This trims wrong-fit clicks.
- Do NOT promise specific savings amounts in ad copy. Compliance + credibility risk.
- Match landing page copy to ad copy. Quality score depends on it.

### Pause rules

The campaign auto-pauses (or you manually pause it) when:

- 7-day click-to-checkout-start rate falls below 3%
- 14-day CPA exceeds $30 (above breakeven on the fee alone)
- Negative-keyword spend exceeds 15% of total spend (means broad-match leakage somewhere)
- Single-keyword wasted spend > $20 with zero checkout starts

## What we are NOT going to do

- **No national generic terms.** "Garage organization", "window replacement", "basement remodel" — these are 50-state queries with national-brand competition. The bidding floor is already $3-8 CPC; the math doesn't work.
- **No retargeting** in the first 30 days. The audience is too small; retargeting frequency caps don't help.
- **No display network.** Display CPM economics combined with $19.99 ACV produce CPC ≥ $1 with ~0.5% CTR. Numbers don't work.
- **No YouTube ads.** Video CPM + cold-traffic conversion rates fail at this price point.
- **No "broad match modifier" or "phrase match"** in initial 30 days. Tighter is better.
- **No automated bidding** in initial 30 days.

## Reporting cadence

- **Daily for first 7 days:** spend, clicks, click-to-checkout-start rate, any keyword > $5 spend with zero conversions
- **Weekly thereafter:** CPA, route-out click rate (paid traffic shouldn't trigger more route-outs than organic — if it does, targeting is wrong), affiliate revenue per cart
- **Monthly:** decide whether to expand match types, geography, or budget — only if 4-week CPA stays below $25

## When to expand

Phase 2 conditions (open phrase match + add second pilot scope's keywords):

- 4 weeks of stable CPA ≤ $20
- Click-to-checkout-start rate ≥ 5% sustained
- No more than 10% of spend on negative-keyword traps
- Affiliate revenue per cart trending up

Phase 3 conditions (test a second geographic state — say New Hampshire or Maine):

- 8 weeks of stable Phase 2 metrics in Vermont
- ≥ 50 carts purchased via paid in Vermont
- Brand-thesis content verified to land in adjacent New England (would need NH/ME-specific guides if expanding)

## Tracking infrastructure

Paid search clicks should land with UTM parameters that map to the existing event log:

- `?utm_source=google` (or bing)
- `?utm_medium=cpc`
- `?utm_campaign=v7-2-14-window-pilot` (or `-basement-pilot`)
- `?utm_content=[ad-variant-id]`
- `?utm_term=[keyword]`

The buyer-event log ([src/lib/buyer-events.ts](../src/lib/buyer-events.ts)) should record the UTM source on `cart_created` so paid vs organic carts are queryable. **Verify before launching paid:** the existing event log captures UTM params on cart creation. If not, add it.

## Human escalation triggers

Paid search needs human review (don't trust automation here) when:

- Daily spend exceeds the cap by ≥ 20%
- Any single keyword spends > $50 with zero checkout starts
- Route-out clicks per session exceeds 1 (paid traffic is mistargeted)
- Refund rate for paid-acquired carts > 2× organic refund rate
- A keyword starts triggering on commercial / B2B variants (means broad-match leak)

## Decision: do we run paid yet?

As of v7.2.14 launch: **NO.** Run organic for 14-30 days first. Re-evaluate with the launch-gates decision rule.

When/if the answer becomes yes, start with **window weatherization only**, $30/day, the 5 exact-match keywords above. Run for 7 days. Read the results. Decide.
